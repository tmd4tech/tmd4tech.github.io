### The problem
Energy operators plan reactively — scheduling maintenance and allocating resources after conditions have already drifted. The signal to act earlier is usually sitting in the operational data; it just isn't being read.

### What I built
A platform that trains machine-learning models on operational time series to forecast trends and surface maintenance needs ahead of time. The forecasts feed directly into scheduling, infrastructure planning, and resource-allocation decisions — moving the operator from reacting to anticipating.

### Methodology
- Framed the core task as multi-horizon time-series forecasting over operational signals.
- Engineered features capturing seasonality, load patterns, and degradation trends.
- Translated model outputs into planning-ready signals — not just predictions, but scheduling and allocation recommendations.
