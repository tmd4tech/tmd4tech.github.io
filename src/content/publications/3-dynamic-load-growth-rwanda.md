A rural health clinic's electricity demand is not static — it grows as services expand, equipment is added, and reliance on the grid deepens. Sizing a microgrid to day-one demand strands the clinic within a few years; oversizing wastes scarce capital. Using a Rwandan case study, we model load growth dynamically and cast microgrid sizing as a multi-stage capacity-expansion problem, staging investment to track demand as it evolves. The approach offers a defensible middle path between under- and over-building critical health infrastructure.
