Agricultural and industrial loads have demand profiles — sharp, seasonal, and productive-use driven — that generic microgrid sizing tools handle poorly. This paper formulates hybrid microgrid design and operation as a mixed-integer linear program, jointly optimizing generation mix and dispatch against those real load shapes. The MILP-based dispatch layer weighs capital cost against operational reliability explicitly, producing configurations tuned to the productive-use electrification case rather than to residential defaults.
